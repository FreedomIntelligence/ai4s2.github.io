Strategic Focus Project | 2026.03 - 2028.03

Economic World Model

A foundation-model and LLM-agent platform for social and market simulation, designed to turn economic systems into controllable, reproducible, and explainable digital sandboxes.

Led by Prof. Lin William Cong, the project defines an Economic World Model as a co-evolving system of agents and environments. It models endogenous market feedback, heterogeneous preferences, institutional rules, and reflexive belief updates so that policies, investment strategies, enterprise decisions, and mechanism designs can be tested before real-world deployment.

Budget RMB 15M excluding compute
Scope Macro Sandbox finance, policy, firms, households
Status Demo Built financial-market prototype validated
01

World-Model Research Stack

  • Economic intelligence base with knowledge injection and rule constraints.
  • Unified environment generation for heterogeneous market and policy settings.
  • Closed-loop co-evolution among people, environments, and AI agents.
  • Scenario validation for financial decision rehearsal and policy simulation.
02

Prototype Economy

  • Heterogeneous households, firms, commercial banks, central bank, and government agents.
  • Loan, labor, goods, stock, and deposit markets with sequential clearing.
  • Macro indicators, policy parameters, and narrative news as shared information environment.
  • Designed to scale from large agent populations toward million-level parallel simulation.
03

Agent Decision Pipeline

  • Observation injects agent attributes and decision-relevant environment signals.
  • Belief generates short market judgments before actions are taken.
  • Price emits structured willingness-to-pay, rate, or valuation outputs.
  • Action selects participation and ratios, then clamps results with economic constraints.

Current Progress

The team has completed an initial financial-market simulation demo, started a public initiative for influence and data partnerships, and is planning an Economic Personality Test mini-game to collect behavioral signals for agent initialization.
