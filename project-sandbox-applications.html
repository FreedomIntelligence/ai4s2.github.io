Applied Tracks

Extending the sandbox across finance, governance, health, and education.

A cross-domain program that adapts world-model infrastructure to decision settings where social outcomes, institutional rules, individual behavior, and AI agents shape one another.

Finance Governance Health Education

Project Logic

One sandbox architecture, multiple social systems.

The applied sandbox program turns AI4S2's core modeling stack into domain-specific digital laboratories. Each laboratory combines heterogeneous agents, structured environments, real-world constraints, visual analytics, and AI assistants so that interventions can be explored before they reach people, markets, or institutions.

The project emphasizes translation from technical capability to social usefulness: model assumptions must be explicit, scenarios must map to real decision processes, and results must be interpretable for domain experts, public-sector partners, and institutional stakeholders.

01

Market and Social Simulation Platform

A foundation platform built on large models, heterogeneous agents, visual analytics, and AI assistants for reproducing interactions among people, organizations, rules, and information environments.

02

Finance and Policy Simulation

Policy shocks, market mechanisms, risk transmission, and institutional strategy testing in settings where regulators, firms, investors, and households respond to changing incentives.

03

Health Management Intelligence

Social-computing approaches for chronic disease management, early warning, personalized intervention agents, and public-health decision support under behavioral and resource constraints.

04

AI Education Digital Twin

Learning agents, digital teachers, simulated students, and education-policy sandboxes for evaluating AI's effects on learning processes, teacher roles, school governance, and education equity.

A

Shared Infrastructure

  • Scenario generation for policy, market, health, and education settings.
  • Agent initialization from domain knowledge, behavioral traces, and expert priors.
  • Monitoring dashboards that connect macro outcomes with micro-level decisions.
B

Domain Adaptation

  • Finance models emphasize incentives, risks, liquidity, and institutional constraints.
  • Governance models emphasize policy objectives, public values, and accountability.
  • Health and education models emphasize human development, service access, and long-term effects.
C

Validation Path

  • Virtual experiments screen interventions before small-scale real-world testing.
  • Expert review checks scenario realism, assumptions, and unintended consequences.
  • Human feedback calibrates agents and updates simulation rules over time.
